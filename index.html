Jimmy Stewart
“If Bess and I had a son, we’d want him to be just like Jimmy Stewart.” - Harry S. Truman
A timeline of Jimmy Stewart's "wonderful" life:
  • 1908 - Born in Indiana, Pennsylvania
  • 1932 - Graduates from Princeton University. With friend Henry Fonda, he travels to New York and lands small roles on Broadway.
  • 1935 - His first film appearance is in The Murder Man starring Spencer Tracy.
  • 1938 - Stars in Frank Capra's You Can't Take It With You with Lionel Barrymore and Jean Arthur
  • 1939 - Wins Best Actor for Mr. Smith Goes to Washington from the New York Film Critics Circle
  • 1940 - Stars in The Philadelphia Story with Katherine Hepburn and Cary Grant
  • 1940 - Enters the service during World War II. He'll go on to earn the Air Medal, the Croix de Guerre, the Distinguished Flying Cross, and seven battle stars.
  • 1941 - Wins the Academy Award for Best Actor for his role in The Philadelphia Story
  • 1947 - In his first film since returning from the war, he portrays the character of George Bailey in Frank Capra's It's a Wonderful Life with Donna Reed
  • 1948 - Stars in the Alfred Hitchcock film, Rope
  • 1949 - Marries former model Gloria Hatrick McLean
  • 1959 - Wins Best Actor for Anatomy of a Murder from the New York Film Critics Circle
  • 1964 - Given the Cecil B. DeMille Award at the Golden Globe Awards
  • 1973 - Wins a Golden Globe for Best Actor-Drama for the TV series Hawkins
  • 1980 - Is awarded the American Film Institute's Lifetime Achievement Award
  • 1994 - His wife of 44 years, Gloria, dies of lung cancer at their home in Beverly Hills
  • 1997 - Stewart dies of cardiac arrest at his home in Beverly Hills at the age of 89. He is surrounded by his children. His last words are: "I'm going to be with Gloria now."
